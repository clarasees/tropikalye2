5_____Etymology
Tropikalye is a blend of two words: 'Tropikal' + 'kalye'
'Tropikal' = Tropical
Filipino for street, loanword from Spanish calle

Tropikalye is a growing vernacular* aesthetic in the Philippines characterized by the ingenious use of low-tech, affordable materials readily available in the provincial-side. While these materials are used for their intended practical purposes, the tropikalye aesthetic is unintentionally formed by a widespread Filipino affinity for invoking lush, paradisiacal landscapes of the Philippine rainforest.

Although often dismissed as "unrefined" by economically privileged Filipinos who are often more attuned to global culture, tropikalye is a unique snapshot representing the visual landscape of rural Philippines.

*vernacular here is celebrated
4____as an aesthetic

Tropikalye explores the aesthetic qualities of everyday Filipino life, particularly those found on the streets. ‘kalye’ here serves to differentiate these elements from those tied to indigenous spaces or cultural institutions, which are already well-preserved and showcased. Tropikalye's true interest lies in the overlooked curiosities, not always outdoors, but embedded in the everyday vernacular.

The informal nature of the term ‘tropikalye’ reflects the organic evolution of this aesthetic, developed often unwittingly and without sponsorship by ordinary people, and influenced by the tropical, humid climate. Unlike a Western art movement, tropikalye does not have a philosophy predefined by an organized group of artists; it exists not as a movement but as a natural aesthetic particular to the Philippine archipelago.

Identifiable characteristics (but tropikalye is not limited to): → Thalo green → Depictions of paraiso/paradise → Faux rocks, wood → Tiled patterns → Almost always juxtaposed with concrete

Common materials used:Thalo green - a particular hue of green explored more on 2.

3___Community

True to its informality, two groups share the same love for the tropikalye, continuing its propagation:

#tropikalye - in an effort to capture, preserve and make sense of the aesthetic, a community index is formed by Nice Buenaventura and Constantino Zicarelli, artists who gave this aesthetic the name tropikalye after noticing similarities of visual sensibilities across provinces.

Green Lover Group (House and Things) - an online community of green lovers, mostly from but not limited to Global South and southeast Asian countries. The group, founded and moderated by Rhealyn Elpozar Saez, a former overseas Filipino worker in Saudi Arabia, various contests of domestic decoration are held.
2__(ph)Thalo green

A defining visual element of tropikalye is this distinctive cool green hue, named Thalo green by the largest local paint manufacturer BOYSEN (Pacific Paint) Philippines. This color holds particular significance for many Filipinos, including the father of this website’s author, who often paint their household walls in this shade as a way to counteract the country’s intense heat. “Malamig sa mata” or cold to the eyes. While there may be no scientific basis to support the idea that this color cools interiors, its popularity persists. The cool green tone also creates visual harmony between interior spaces and the lush Philippine foliage visible through windows, seamlessly blending indoors with the natural outdoors.

This interplay of color and nature speaks to a deeper, almost intuitive connection between the built environment and the tropical landscape, underscoring how aesthetics in everyday life often respond to practical needs while maintaining a seamless dialogue with the surrounding natural world. In a hyperglobalized world, Thalo is green is an authentic local visual expression that we can say is truly Filipino.

As contrast, homes found in gated villages in the capital Manila allude to minimalism- white, rigid. These are neighborhoods that are more in touch with imported media and visual trends.
1_Application

There are numerous examples of the tropikalye coming into play. Here are two applications to showcase the variety of ways tropikalye been present in contemporary Philippines:



Upcycled Mountain Dew Bottles beautiful improvisation of single-use plastic in the form of sculpting acid green, Mountain Dew bottles, to either the form of palm trees, Christmas trees. Also popularly used to create eye-catching typography signages



Chel Diokno senatorial campaign Chel Diokno is a prominent Filipino lawyer and public figure known for his work in promoting justice and defending human rights in the Philippines. His visual identity for senatorial candidacy in the previous 2022 national elections used a mint green color chosen to represent ‘something fresh’ garnered significant support, particularly among younger voters.



Seattle craftsman-style home in Thalo green - a marker for Filipino household, the Christmas wreath still up on the front door in July is added bonus.
